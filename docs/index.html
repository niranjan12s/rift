01. Overview

The Physics of Meaning

Transformers revolutionized AI by using "attention" to let tokens in a sequence reference each other. However, this still treats language as a sequence of isolated, discrete symbols parsed sequentially.

The RIFT architecture argues that language is fluid. It builds on the intuition that a sentence is a dynamic system—like ripples in a pond. Rather than relying entirely on pair-wise attention to bind tokens together, RIFT creates sentence-level meaning through wave interference.

The Core Hypothesis

By mapping tokens into wavelets (with amplitude, frequency, and phase) and allowing them to physically "interfere" with one another, we can encode the structural rhythm and internal resonance of a sentence directly into the math, creating a holistic semantic wave prior to surface generation.

02. How it Works

The Generative Pipeline

RIFT introduces a novel approach to composition, distributing parameters into specialized submodules that handle frequency-based reasoning rather than standard opaque attention weights.

1. Wavelet Emission

Instead of mapping a word to a static vector, each token is mapped to a multi-dimensional wavelet representation, parameterized by amplitude, frequency, and phase.

2. Sentence Composition

The individual token wavelets are aggregated to compose a single, complex sentence-level semantic wave. This wave embodies the distributed meaning of the entire context.

03. Exploratory Results

Resilience in Low-Data Regimes

To evaluate RIFT, we compared it against a compact Transformer baseline (roughly ~125k-142k parameters each). The models were subjected to an extreme low-data environment: trained on only 100 sentences with a vocabulary cap of 200 tokens.

The <unk> Collapse

Under these extreme constraints, the standard Transformer completely collapsed, outputting endless streams of unknown tokens and repetitive loops. RIFT, however, avoided this collapse, producing grammatically loose but lexically diverse attempts at structural composition.

Generation Metric Transformer Baseline RIFT Model
1-gram Diversity 0.0938 0.7775
3-gram Diversity 0.1200 1.0000
Repetition Score 0.9902 (High Repetition) 0.3925 (Low Repetition)

Note: These metrics reflect specific exploratory runs under constrained environments. Due to GPU compute limitations, we were unable to run standardized large-scale benchmarks like WikiText-2 (WT2). It is well established that Transformers perform poorly in extremely low-data regimes, so these results cannot conclusively prove RIFT is superior to Transformers at any stage. Rather, this serves as an exploration into the feasibility of alternative, structure-driven generative architectures.
